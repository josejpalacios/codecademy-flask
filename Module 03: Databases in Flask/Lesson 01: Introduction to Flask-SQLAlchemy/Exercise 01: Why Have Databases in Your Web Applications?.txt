Relational databases: Consists of tables that represent entities and/or relationships amongst entities. 
Database schema: The way a database is organized in entities, attributes and relationships, without data being present.
Entities: Represent tables in the schema.
Attributes: Properties of an entity and are represented as columns in a database table.
Relationships: One-to-many or one-to-one.
Primary Key: Uniquely identify entries in the table.
Foreign Key: References to Primary Keys of other tables.
